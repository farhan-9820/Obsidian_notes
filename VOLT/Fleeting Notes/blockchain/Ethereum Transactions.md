A transaction is a digitally signed message broadcast to the network. If Accounts are the nouns of Ethereum, Transactions are the verbs.

Transactions can transfer Ether or invoke smart contract code, requiring sufficient balance to cover fees. 

Every standard Ethereum transaction contains these core components:

- `Recipient (To)`: The target Ethereum address (either a human or a smart contract).
    
- `Signature (From)`: The cryptographic proof of who sent it.
    
- `Value`: The exact amount of ETH being transferred.
    
- `Data (Optional Payload)`: The instructions sent to a smart contract (e.g., _"execute function 'Swap' with parameter 'USDC'"_).
    
- `Gas Limit` & `Max Fee`: The maximum computational power allocated for the transaction, and what the sender is willing to pay for it.
