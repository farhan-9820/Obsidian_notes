Before blockchains even existed, cryptographer Nick Szabo conceptualized smart contracts using the theoretical model of a **vending machine**.

A vending machine is a primitive hardware smart contract:

- **The Rule:** "If you input $2.00 AND press button A1, then dispense a soda."
    
- **The Enforcement:** The machine uses mechanical and electronic rules to enforce the agreement. It does not require a cashier, a lawyer, or a judge to mediate. If the conditions are met, the outcome is _guaranteed_.
    

In blockchain theory, a smart contract is simply a digital version of this vending machine, but instead of physical gears, it uses cryptographic math.
