### The Three Types of Blockchains

Buterin categorizes blockchains into three distinct models based on who is allowed to read, write, and participate in consensus:

- **Public Blockchains:** Fully decentralized and permissionless (e.g., Bitcoin, Ethereum). Anyone can read, send transactions, and participate in the consensus process (secured by cryptoeconomics like Proof of Work or Proof of Stake).
    
- **Consortium Blockchains:** Hybrid networks where the consensus process is controlled by a pre-selected set of nodes (e.g., a group of 15 financial institutions). It splits the difference between the low trust of public systems and the single-entity trust of private ones.
    
- **Fully Private Blockchains:** Entirely centralized write permissions handled by a single organization. Read permissions can be public or restricted. These function more like highly audited internal databases.
