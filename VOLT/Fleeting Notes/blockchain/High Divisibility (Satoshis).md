## High Divisibility (The Power of "Satoshis")

When people think of currency, they often think of whole units (like 1 Dollar or 1 Bitcoin). But Bitcoin was designed from day one to be broken down into tiny fractions.

One Bitcoin can be divided down to 8 decimal places. The smallest unit of Bitcoin is called a **Satoshi** (or "Sat").

- $1 \text{ BTC} = 100,000,000 \text{ Satoshis}$
    

Because there are 100 million Satoshis in just _one_ Bitcoin, the total supply of spendable units is actually **2.1 quadrillion Satoshis** ($2,100,000,000,000,000$).

### How this scales with demand:

If the global population grows and demand for Bitcoin skyrockets, the _purchasing power_ of a single Bitcoin increases. Instead of buying a cup of coffee with `0.0001 BTC`, you might buy it with `5 Satoshis`. People won't be pricing goods in full Bitcoins; they will be pricing them in Satoshis.

> **Fun Fact:** If Bitcoin ever needs to be divided even further in the future (beyond 8 decimal places), the network can vote to update the code to allow for micro-Satoshis. The 21 million cap stays the same, but the slices of the pie can get infinitely smaller
