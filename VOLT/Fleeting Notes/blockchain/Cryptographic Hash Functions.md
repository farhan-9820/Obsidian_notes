These are designed to be impossible to reverse-engineer or tamper with.

- **SHA-2 (Secure Hash Algorithm 2):** Designed by the NSA. The most famous variant is **SHA-256**, which generates a 256-bit (64 character) hash. It is the backbone of the Bitcoin blockchain and securing SSL/TLS web traffic.
    
- **SHA-3:** The newest member of the SHA family. It uses a completely different internal structure (called a sponge construction) for added security against future quantum computing threats. It is used heavily in the Ethereum blockchain.
    
- **MD5 & SHA-1:** These are older functions that are now **deprecated (obsolete)**. Computers became powerful enough to find "collisions" (generating the same hash from two different inputs), making them unsafe for security, though they are still occasionally used to check for accidental file corruption.
