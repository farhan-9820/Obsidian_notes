## The Significance Level ($\alpha$)

The **Significance Level** (or "Alpha") is the probability of making a **Type I Error** (rejecting the null hypothesis when it is actually true). It is the risk you are willing to take of being wrong.

### The Equation:

$$\alpha + \text{Confidence Level} = 1$$

### Breaking Down the Math:

- **$\alpha$:** Usually set at $0.05$ ($5\%$).
    
- **$1$:** Represents $100\%$ total probability.
    

**Simple English Translation:**

"If I want to be 95% confident in my results, I have to accept a 5% chance that I might be seeing a 'fluke' or a random coincidence. That 5% risk is my significance score."
